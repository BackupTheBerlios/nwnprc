New Crafting System

back to Players.

The Player Resource Consortium also includes a new crafting system. This is not designed to replace Biowares crafting of potions, scrolls and wands; instead it is intended for more general magic item crafting.

 

This is still in BETA. It is not a final working system.

 

Some things to know about the whole design of the magic crafting system:

1. It was not designed for the single player to use on a module that does not support it. In order to make a magic item you have to already have the magic item, and its recipe needs to be included into the proper 2da file.

2. The system was designed to near perfectly simulate the DM + player bartering that goes on when a player asks the DM, "Hey, can my wizard make item X?" If the DM agrees, this is like them making the recipe and putting it into the 2da file. Then the DM creates the scroll with the recipe and gives that scroll to the player. The player then collects all the required stuff to make it and then makes the item.

3. It allows the PW DMs to ensure only certain items can be made. Players can't craft anything without you giving players the recipes, or having the item to recipe 2da file filled in.

4. As the system is designed its just won't work without you making a recipe and then giving your PC that recipe scroll. So in the long run you are better off making something in the toolset and then giving that item to your PC and not even bothering with the crafting system except to enforce that your character at least has the feats to do it legally.

5. But  that totally sucks for single players! Yup, but look at it this way. If you played HotU you had the chance to make a weapon with 10+ weapon properties on it and once you got that weapon everything in your path just fell over. If the crafting system was able to introduce items that the module author or the PW didn't want you could break the module hard. The system as it is done requires the module builder or the PW to take action and offer the player magic item recipes that they approve of.

6. But shouldn't the cost of the magic items balance out the value of the item itself? No, cost is only half the equation time is the other, and since in single player it makes no sense to leave your computer on overnight while your pc makes something (i.e. the time has no impact on the player), 1/2 of the cost to the single player is gone.

7. So the single player is out of luck then? Well.... no, this is the first run of the system, an alpha test. If there is enough interest, and other people start making recipe 2das. I can come up with a dialog based recipe book, that is giving to the player when they take any of the craft magic item feats. This will give the single player a way to select which item they want to craft and generate the scroll for them. Then they craft from the scroll. It will have the possibility of doing great harm to low magic modules, but you could already bust those kind of modules with a fully magic decked out PC anyway.
