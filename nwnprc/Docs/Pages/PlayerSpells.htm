Also included in the PRC is Boneshanks Epic Spellcasting System.

This is a builder based system, meaning that it will only work in module that is designed to work with it.

The system itself works quite seamlessly. All the spells are based upon certain spell Seeds, (such as Energy, Armor, etc) and to gain a seed a caster needs to find a book containing information on that Seed. The book is then used via a Unique Power property, and a feat is added to the character. This feat shows in the character sheet, so a player can tell what Seeds they do and do not know.

Once a player has some Seeds, they need to find a Spell Book. There is one spell book per epic spell, each spell book has a Unique Power property which will tell the player what the effects of the spell are, what seeds it requires, and what difficulty it is. The more powerful spells have a harder DC (Difficulty Check) associated with them, ranging from the mid 20's, to over 100. Some feats can reduce the DC of a spell, for example a School Focus feat decreases the DC of epic spells from that school.

When a player has a Spell Book, they have to research the spell. To research a spell, place the spell book onto a research lab (or an altar, or whatever the builder has designed). All spells cost an amount of gold and XP to research, this is proportional to the DC of the spell. If the player has the required seeds, enough gold and XP, they will make a spellcraft check against the DC of the spell. If this is successful, the gold and XP is lost and the player gains a "researched spell" feat. If the check is unsuccessful, the player looses a proportion of the gold, and none of the XP.

After a spell has been successfully researched, the caster needs to place it onto their epic spell radial. To allow this, when resting a conversation will appear with an option to change epic spell radials. This can be accessed at any time outside of combat, and you do not have to rest to change your epic spells. One restriction however, is that you cannot have more than 7 spells on the radial. This is a Bioware bug, nothing we can do about it!

When you rest, you refresh a number of Epic Spell Slots equal to your lore skill divided by 10 (rounding down).  To cast a spell, simply select it from the radial. After the spell has been cast and the projectile impacts, you will have to make a spellcraft check against the DC of the spell. If you succeed, the spell takes effect, if you fail, they spell is wasted. Whenever you attempt to cast a spell, successfully or not, you use up one of your Epic Spell Slots for that day.
